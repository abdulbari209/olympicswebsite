BIOGRAPHY

Leon Marchand: France’s Swimming Phenom

Leon Marchand, born on May 17, 2002, in Toulouse, France, is one of the brightest young stars in competitive swimming. Coming from a family of swimmers—his father, Xavier Marchand, was a world-class medley swimmer—Leon was destined for greatness in the pool. He began making waves early in his career, quickly establishing himself as France’s top medley swimmer. His talent was evident from a young age, and by his teenage years, he was already competing at the highest levels of international swimming. Marchand’s breakthrough came at the 2022 World Aquatics Championships in Budapest, where he stunned the swimming world by winning gold in both the 200-meter and 400-meter individual medley (IM). In the 400 IM, he delivered one of the most dominant performances in recent history, setting a new European record and coming within striking distance of Michael Phelps' long-standing world record. His versatility and endurance in multiple strokes made him a formidable competitor, solidifying his reputation as one of the best all-around swimmers in the world. In 2023, Marchand continued his meteoric rise, breaking Phelps' legendary 400 IM world record at the World Championships in Fukuoka. His time of 4:02.50 shattered expectations and marked the end of an era dominated by Phelps. With his impeccable technique, powerful turns, and strategic race execution, Marchand has positioned himself as a major contender for multiple gold medals at the Paris 2024 Olympics. His ability to perform under pressure and his rapid improvement have made him a standout athlete in the sport. Outside of competition, Marchand has trained under the guidance of legendary coach Bob Bowman, who also coached Michael Phelps. This partnership has helped him refine his skills and prepare for the biggest stages in swimming. As France’s top swimming hope for the Paris Olympics, he carries the expectations of a nation eager to see him shine. With his relentless drive and extraordinary talent, Leon Marchand is set to leave an indelible mark on the history of swimming.
